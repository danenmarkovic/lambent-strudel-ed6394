Case studies

01

Evidence-to-market plan for a specialty healthcare product

A regional life science team was preparing partner and advisory discussions for a specialist product, with evidence, claims and commercial assumptions spread across different documents.

Bion Med role: Bion Med built an evidence map by stakeholder group, separated strong evidence from assumptions, prepared a message matrix for medical and business discussions, identified missing proof points and converted the material into a 90-day evidence-to-market action plan.

Practical work delivered: Evidence landscape, stakeholder question list, value-story structure, objection log, advisory discussion guide and a launch-readiness checklist for medical, market access and commercial teams.

Business outcome: The client entered external discussions with a clearer product narrative, a prioritized evidence plan and a practical internal roadmap for partner engagement, prelaunch preparation and cross-functional execution.

02

CRO selection and SOP-enabled clinical operations setup

A sponsor-side team was preparing a multi-country clinical operations activity and needed to compare CRO proposals, governance models, timelines and delivery risks.

Bion Med role: Bion Med prepared the partner-selection logic, weighted scorecard, RFP questions, governance model and risk register. In parallel, operational SOP flows were translated into practical working steps for feasibility follow-up, document tracking, action logs, issue escalation and meeting governance.

Practical work delivered: CRO comparison matrix, decision pack for internal approval, project governance calendar, responsibility map, escalation pathway and simple automation-ready trackers for status, documents, risks and follow-up actions.

Business outcome: The client received a more transparent partner decision process, fewer unclear handovers between internal and external teams and a more audit-friendly operational structure before project start.

03

Medical device launch readiness and field execution model

A medical device team was moving from preparation to market-facing execution, with claims, distributor expectations, training materials and feedback loops not yet aligned.

Bion Med role: Bion Med connected scientific, regulatory, commercial and operational inputs into a launch-readiness view. The work focused on claims discipline, evidence-to-claim logic, target-account prioritization, field team preparation and a practical process for capturing customer feedback after launch.

Practical work delivered: Launch-readiness checklist, claims and evidence matrix, target-account logic, distributor briefing structure, internal training outline, customer inquiry flow and post-launch feedback tracker.

Business outcome: The client obtained clearer go/no-go gates, stronger consistency between product evidence and field communication, and a more realistic execution plan for prelaunch, launch and early post-launch follow-up.

04

SOP automation for internal and external business processes

A healthcare business unit had repeated manual work across partner follow-up, meeting actions, document control, inquiry handling and management reporting.

Bion Med role: Bion Med reviewed the current operating flow, identified repetitive decision points, converted key activities into SOP-based process steps and proposed a practical automation structure that could be used without disrupting regulated documentation principles.

Practical work delivered: Process map, SOP step logic, responsibility matrix, automated follow-up tracker, standardized inquiry intake, meeting action register, status dashboard structure and escalation rules for delayed or incomplete actions.

Business outcome: The client received a clearer and more repeatable operating model, reduced manual coordination burden and improved visibility of responsibilities, timelines and open risks across internal and external stakeholders.

05

Portfolio prioritization and business development preparation

A regional life sciences business unit needed to evaluate product and partnership opportunities across therapeutic relevance, evidence maturity, regulatory complexity and business contribution.

Bion Med role: Bion Med created a structured portfolio assessment model, challenged the assumptions behind each opportunity and translated scientific, market and operational inputs into a decision-ready view for leadership discussion.

Practical work delivered: Portfolio prioritization scorecard, opportunity profiles, evidence and access gap list, partner discussion points, risk-benefit view and phased execution plan for the most attractive opportunities.

Business outcome: The client gained a more disciplined way to decide where to focus time, resources and partner conversations, with clearer logic for what should move forward, what should wait and what required additional validation.
